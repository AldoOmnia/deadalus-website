Daedalus was the master craftsman of Greek mythology — the architect of the labyrinth, the builder of wings. Both things at once: the system that contains, and the intelligence that escapes it.

Our logo is a factory floor, viewed from above, depicted as a labyrinth.

Today every intelligent layer built for the factory floor talks to machines. Daedalus talks to the worker wearing wearables: capturing what PLCs cannot see, reasoning over what the whole system knows, and building the world model that makes it permanent.

Every factory AI play missed the same thing

The machine-side is covered. PLCs are connected. Sensors stream data. AI agents run on the edge and predict failures, configure equipment, optimize throughput. None of it has solved the human layer.

The worker building the product — assembling the part, picking the component, running the procedure — generates data that no PLC can capture. Their hands are the most important sensor in the facility. Until now, that sensor had no interface.

Tablets and phones extended the desktop paradigm to the floor. They did not replace it. Glasses are the first interface designed for the work itself — always-on, hands-free, spatially aware, worn during assembly, not instead of it. But we are not another glasses company. We are a spatial intelligence company. The wearable is the instrument.

The integrations it requires — MES, WMS, SCADA, procedures — become the platform.

The data it collects becomes the world model.

MCP Intelligent layer
